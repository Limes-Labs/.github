# Code of Conduct

Limes Labs is building public infrastructure for European open AI work. We need a contributor environment that is rigorous, respectful, and usable by researchers, engineers, policy contributors, institutions, and newcomers.

## Expected behavior

- Be precise about facts, assumptions, and uncertainty.
- Critique ideas, methods, evidence, and claims rather than people.
- Welcome good-faith questions and requests for clarification.
- Give credit for sources, prior work, and contributions.
- Keep disagreement focused on improving the work.
- Respect privacy, confidentiality, and legal constraints.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, intimidation, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or conduct.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Posting credentials, secrets, exploit details, or sensitive institutional information.
- Repeatedly derailing issues or pull requests away from concrete project work.
- Misrepresenting project status, affiliation, capability, or access to resources.

## Scope

This code applies in Limes Labs repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, project spaces, and project-related communication channels.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns to hello@limeslabs.eu with the subject line `[conduct]`. Include links, screenshots, or a concise description when possible.

Maintainers will review reports, may ask for more context, and may take action such as warnings, comment moderation, temporary restrictions, or removal from project spaces.

## Enforcement principles

Actions should be proportionate, documented when appropriate, and focused on protecting contributors and the integrity of the work. Maintainers may remove content that exposes sensitive information while preserving enough context to resolve the issue privately.
